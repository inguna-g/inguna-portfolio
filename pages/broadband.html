Broadband Signup Flow

Reducing drop-off and improving customer satisfaction during signup

Tl;Dr

I led the UX research efforts on a multi-step broadband signup flow, helping reduce drop-off by 9% and decrease support requests. This was a long-term, iterative project focused on optimizing a complex customer journey.

The Story

Tackling a Complex, Evolving Signup Journey

Analyzed comprehensive data (analytics, heatmaps, customer service feedback) to identify key friction points and high abandonment rates across a 10+ screen signup process.

Collaborated with stakeholders in quarterly meetings to monitor KPIs and pinpoint user pain points, especially concerning continuous product changes.

Optimizing Authentication & Upsells

Designed and extensively tested variations for a mandated strong authentication step. My focus was on optimizing its placement and microcopy to build user trust and avoid confusion (a common issue in Latvia where users confused it with contract signing).

Iterated on upsell integration, strategically placing add-on products and refining microcopy so users perceived them as valuable, not intrusive.

Designing for Clarity: Progress Visualization

Led the design and testing of various progress bar visualizations to guide users through the lengthy flow.

Solved for dynamic step counts, ensuring the progress indicator remained clear and prevented disorientation even when the number of steps changed based on user choices.

Rigorous, Iterative Usability Research Methodology

As part of a two-person UX research team, I developed and conducted rigorous usability testing. This included creating interactive prototypes (often two variants per flow), meticulously crafting test scenarios, and defining tasks for moderated 'think-aloud' video calls.

Coded and analyzed video recordings post-testing, extracting key highlights and user quotes. I then presented data-driven findings to stakeholders, providing clear answers to questions and guiding subsequent iterations. Our work consistently ensured optimized solutions without requiring revolutionary overhauls.
