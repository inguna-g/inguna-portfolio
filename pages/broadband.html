Broadband Signup Flow

Reducing drop-off and improving customer satisfaction during signup

Key Moments

  • Identified friction in address entry
  • Simplified bundle selection flow
  • Improved clarity on plan details

Tl;Dr

A UX optimization of the signup flow for broadband plans reduced drop-off by 9% and decreased support requests. Optimizing this flow was a long-term focus for me as part of the UX Research team.

The Story

Continuous monitoring of KPI's

At least once every quarter we held meetings with stakeholders to determine what changes needed to be implemented in the sign-up flow next.
The product was dynamically evolving: adding bundles, legal changes, price, availability, and service type were all subject to change.
Our goal was to ensure changes were implemented in a way that would lead to decreased customer drop-off.

Usability tests

For each iteration, we completed a full usability research cycle. From interactive prototypes, defining questions, recruiting participants, to analyzing results and presenting actionable suggestions for the next design iteration.

Results varied

Considering it was an ongoing project, the results varied each cycle. Our aim was to keep the most important customer signup flow performing at full capacity.
Our best results showed a decrease in abandonment, a drop in related support calls, and improved NPS scores.
