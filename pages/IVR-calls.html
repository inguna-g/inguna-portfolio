Interactive voice response (IVR) optimization

Solving misrouted calls and enhancing customer support efficiency.

Tl;Dr

I led a comprehensive optimization project for our Interactive Voice Response (IVR) system. This involved auditing a year's worth of customer call data, conducting stakeholder interviews, and implementing a rigorous pre- and post-testing methodology using closed card sorting in Treejack.

The validated solution achieved an estimated ~30% reduction in incorrectly placed calls, significantly reducing overhead for our call center's technical support team and improving customer satisfaction.

The Story

Uncovering the Hidden Problem

Business analysts and call center management identified a critical, costly issue: one-third of calls routed to "technical issues" were actually about something else. This led to increased wait times and frustrated customers.

Our goal was clear: redefine IVR options to reduce misrouted calls and boost call center efficiency. We quickly realized a key culprit was billing information being on a separate, less obvious number.

As part of a three-person design team, I lead the testing and validation process, and presented the results to stakeholders.

Phase 1: Existing IVR Tree Analysis & Data Mapping

I conducted a deep dive into the current IVR, including content analysis of scripts, competitor benchmarks, and a thorough review of quantitative call data.

Phase 2: Benchmarking the Current IVR with Treejack

I designed and set up the entire Treejack study to benchmark the current IVR structure using a closed card sorting methodology. This included writing survey questions and preparing the card sorting task.

I collaborated closely with call center colleagues to integrate their insights and operational requirements into our research.

Phase 3: Designing & Validating the Optimized IVR

I contributed to designing the "perfect" IVR tree by:

  • Renaming ambiguous categories for intuition
  • Consolidating redundant options for simplicity.
  • Introducing new, user-centric categories (e.g., clear billing options).
  • Optimizing the sequence of options for a better flow.

My team and I rigorously tested this proposed solution using closed card sorting in Treejack, balancing task complexity with result validity.

I presented validated findings and the refined IVR structure to stakeholders, detailing expected improvements.
