Tl;Dr

For this EU-funded pilot, I designed the prototype for a clinical app aimed at helping seniors track grip strength ‚Äî an early health decline indicator. We aligned with medical constraints, adapted patterns from fitness and meditation apps, and usability-tested the Dutch-language prototype with seniors through guerilla testing, workshops, and in-person sessions.

The Story

Understanding Constraints

The client provided detailed academic research defining test type, sequence, rest time, etc. My job was to shape that into a usable interface. I mapped the relationships and flow, and explored parallels from existing validated UI patterns ‚Äî including heart-rate and meditation apps.

Rapid Prototyping & Testing

I created a clickable Dutch prototype and ran guerilla testing on friends and family to catch basic interaction issues. Then, I prepped a usability script and led a roleplay-based workshop to train stakeholders on running tests with seniors.

In-Person & Remote Testing

Stakeholders ran tests with geriatric patients ‚Äî either face-to-face or via video call ‚Äî using my script and guidance. We refined instructions, added reassuring microcopy, and introduced a visual progress indicator to show completion.
