UniWeb EDC Platform

Designing a SaaS Electronic Data Capture System for Clinical Trials

The Challenge

UniWeb, my employer, had an existing Electronic Data Capture (EDC) platform that was custom-built for a long-term client, DICE-CRO (a data investigation company specializing in clinical trials). Our leadership envisioned a new, scalable SaaS-based EDC system that would propel UniWeb to new heights in the clinical trial market. This initiative aimed to transform a bespoke solution into a reusable, cutting-edge product.

My Role

As the first and sole UX designer on this ambitious project, I spearheaded the design efforts for approximately one year. My responsibilities encompassed the entire UX lifecycle, from initial concept to detailed prototyping. I focused on:

  • Requirement Gathering: Collaborating to define the core needs for the new SaaS platform.
  • Use-Case Diagramming: Translating complex clinical trial workflows into clear use-case diagrams.
  • Interactive Prototyping: Creating comprehensive and fully interactive prototypes using Axure RP 8.
  • Documentation: Ensuring all design and research documentation adhered to Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP), crucial for the regulated clinical trials environment.
  • Guerrilla Testing: Conducting rapid, informal usability tests on various parts of the prototypes to gather immediate feedback.
  • Design System Development: Simultaneously building a robust design system, primarily based on Google Material Design, which was a specific mandate from leadership. This choice proved beneficial given the form-heavy nature of EDC systems.

Key Features of the EDC Platform

The SaaS EDC system was designed to be a comprehensive solution for managing clinical trial data, featuring:

  • Data Entry: Real-time validation, dynamic visits, fields, and forms.
  • Monitoring: Full and partial (targeted approach) source data verification.
  • Data Review: Capability to generate queries and access a range of default reports.
  • Data Extraction: Generated customized clinical data sets.
  • Administration: Robust user and site management, trial configuration, and user permissions.
  • Audit Trail: A full audit trail for both subject and non-subject data, ensuring compliance and data integrity.
  • Randomization
  • Double Data Entry
  • Training modules.
  • Subject PDF generation

Approach & Challenges

This project was an internal initiative, which meant it often took a backseat to work for paying customers. Despite these prioritization challenges, I remained committed to delivering a high-quality product. The usability of the system was paramount, as the validity and integrity of clinical trial data literally depended on it.

Outcome & Impact (Unlaunched)

I transitioned from UniWeb before the SaaS EDC platform was officially launched, and to my knowledge, it never went live. However, the design work was completed with clear objectives in mind:

  • Improved Data Entry Speed: Streamlining workflows and reducing friction for users.
  • Increased User Satisfaction: Creating an intuitive and less error-prone experience.
  • Reduced Errors and Re-work: Enhancing data quality and operational efficiency.
  • Enhanced Reusability: Designing the platform to require little or no custom coding for each new clinical trial instance, significantly cutting down deployment time and costs.

Although the project's ultimate fate is unknown to me, I am proud of the foundational UX work that set the stage for a potentially transformative product in the clinical trials space, emphasizing usability and data integrity above all.
